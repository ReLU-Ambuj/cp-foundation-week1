# Day 7: Speed, Clean Code, and Mini Project Integration

## Day Objective
To transition from merely "solving" isolated algorithmic problems to writing **production-quality, structured logic** under extreme time pressure. Today, you integrate everything you've learned into a modular CLI application.

## Why Speed Without Clarity is Dangerous
In CP, a 50-line spaghetti code solution might pass the tests, but if it has a bug, you will spend 30 minutes reading your own unreadable variable names trying to fix it. *Clarity is Speed.* Writing clean, decoupled functions the *first time* prevents catastrophic debugging sessions later.

## Hour-by-Hour Execution Plan
- **Hour 0–3**: Speed Re-Solve (20 Problems strictly under 10 mins each).
- **Hour 3–5**: Optimization & Refactoring Review (Studying DRY, Single Responsibility, and redundant loops).
- **Hour 5–10**: Mini Project — Building the strictly modular Console Utility Suite.
- **Hour 10–12**: Testing & final repository audit.

## Strict Day 7 Rules
- **No copying old solutions.**
- **No looking at previous days' code.** You must generate logic from your own internalized memory.
- **Max 10 minutes per re-solve.** If you hit 10:00, you drop it and move to the next.
- **Refactoring Philosophy**: A function does exactly *one* thing. No global state.

At the end of Day 7, you will audit your entire Week 1 repository using the `final_push_checklist.md`, commit your final mini-project, and officially close out the CP Foundation Week 1 milestone.\n
