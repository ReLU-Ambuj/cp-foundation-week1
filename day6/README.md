# Day 6: Consolidation Marathon & Mock Test Simulation

## Day Objective
To abandon the safety of isolated theory and simulate **Real Contest Pressure**. Today, you will combine variables, loops, conditionals, strings, hash maps, and functions under strict time limits. You will experience stress, blanking out, and stupid syntax errors. That is the point.

## Why Consolidation Matters
Knowing how a `while` loop works is useless if you cannot write it flawlessly while a ticking clock screams at you. Consolidation turns raw knowledge into "Contest Reflex."

## How to Simulate Contest Environment
- **No music.**
- **No phone.**
- **No Googling syntax.**
- **No IDE Autocomplete.**
- If you forget how to append to a list, try until it works or skip the problem.

## The Marathon (20 Problems)
- **Format**: 20 rapid-fire problems covering Days 1-5.
- **Rule**: Max 15 minutes per problem. Strict.
- **Skip Strategy**: If a problem takes $>5$ minutes just to mentally model, skip it. You must secure easy points first.
- **Goal**: Speed and unbroken momentum. No breaks during the marathon.

## The Mock Test (10 Problems, 2 Hours Strict)
- Read `mock_test/instructions.md` before starting.
- You have exactly 120 minutes to solve 10 custom, multi-concept problems.
- Track every minute. 

## Post-Test Analysis Protocol
After the mock test, you must brutally audit yourself:
1. Fill out the `marathon_tracker.md`.
2. Map your errors in `mistake_classification.md`.
3. Score your weaknesses in the `weak_spot_matrix.md`.

## Commit Strategy
Do not commit after every problem today.
Commit once after finishing the 20 Marathon problems.
